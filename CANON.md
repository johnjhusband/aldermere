# Aldermere — Canon Bible

Source of truth for the world's lore — characters, factions, locations, recurring patterns, and story-arc templates. Every new Aldermere world starts seeded from this document. The AI adapts the canon (renames, retunes, recasts) per the Governor's setup interview, but the **roles**, **dynamics**, and **patterns** here persist.

Story lead: **Maria Harrell**. The fictional Editor of every Gazette is also named **Maria** (real Maria has approved this).

---

## I. Kingdom-Wide Story Factions

These are the larger ongoing storylines that show up in the Gazette and bleed into daily chores.

### 1. Animal Rights Coalition

- **Function in the world:** The growing political movement of woodland creatures and animal workers demanding recognition, fair treatment, representation, and eventually fair pay.
- **Household tie-in:** Bed-making. Mice and other animal helpers have suspended bedding services during labor disputes. Citizens maintain their own quarters until further notice.
- **Story role:** Ongoing long-form kingdom plot — begins with murmurs of labor concerns, progresses through protests, hearings, negotiations, treaties. The strike does **not** resolve quickly; it should feel like a true political movement unfolding in stages over months.
- **Characters:** No named leader (intentional — keeps the movement feeling broader than one spokesperson).

### 2. Porcelain Republic

- **Function:** The governing body responsible for the Great Basin (the sink), dish traffic, washing logistics, and kitchen-cleanup trade routes.
- **Household tie-in:** Rinse dishes before placing in the basin · Unload dishwasher · Hand-wash specialty dishes (the "artisan" task) · Load dishwasher.
- **Story role:** Recurring storylines involving embargoes, inspections, shipping slowdowns, basin disputes.
- **Characters:** No fixed named official (Republic stays bureaucratic).

### 3. Deep Archives of the Vault

- **Function:** The kingdom's archive / storage / basement, site of a recurring curse that causes disorder and chaos.
- **Household tie-in:** Basement organization, periodic deep clean, seasonal decluttering.
- **Story role:** "Special event" department. Used to mentally prepare citizens for heavier tasks before they arrive; should be foreshadowed in the Gazette before the actual task day.
- **Recurring notes:** The Vault is cursed. The curse causes it to descend into chaos roughly every few months. Great for Gazette buildup — rustling in lower sectors, mislabeled crates, holiday decor inside gardening bins, archivists pretending everything is normal.
- **Characters:** None named yet (a stressed archivist would fit).

## II. Daily Household Departments / Guilds

### 4. Laundry Guild

- **Function:** Kingdom laundering, textile sorting, washing, drying, folding, garment return.
- **Household tie-in:** Five-step daily chain — Sort → Start (begin wash) → Transfer (to dryer) → Fold/Hang → Completion event (citizens claim their garments).
- **Rhythm:** Laundry is a **five-day task, not seven**. The reason for needing help changes daily (staff at the Sheep Shearing Festival, hiccup plague, jury duty, etc.) but the chain structure stays familiar.
- **Day-of-week end ritual:** Day 5 closes with Mabel ringing the collection bell from atop the Guild Hall, citizens arriving to claim freshly cleaned garments, and possibly a formal commendation from Mabel for the player.
- **Characters:**
  - **Head Laundress Mabel** — runs the guild. Competent, dependable, appreciative of help. Main quest-giver.
  - **Foreman Phelps** — oversees transport between washing/drying stages. Usually the second stage of the chain.
  - **Faye** — newest recruit. Frequently needs training on folding and hanging. Great recurring "improving rookie."

### 5. Royal Culinary Society

- **Function:** Represents the kingdom's inns, taverns, tea houses, and eateries — how meals are framed inside Aldermere.
- **Household tie-in:** Breakfast · Lunch · Dinner.
- **Story role:** **Not a quest chain.** It is a meal / energy section.
- **System rule:** The Royal Culinary Society does **NOT** award Gold, Skill, or Strength. **Only Energy.**
- **Daily structure:**
  - One featured restaurant / inn / tavern
  - Short lore about that establishment
  - Checkboxes for breakfast, lunch, dinner
  - Energy values next to each meal
- **Known restaurants:**
  - **The Copper Kettle Tavern** — where King Ferdian beat the Duke of Shambles in an arm-wrestling match before the Battle of Armory. Three separate tables claim to be the historic table; no one agrees which is real.
  - **The Golden Goose Inn** — established 1269. Famous golden weather vane has been stolen seven times. Each thief returned it within a week. No explanation has ever been offered.
  - **The Wandering Cauldron** — popular with witches. Officially a place for magical research gatherings. Unofficially, very likely a book club and gossip.
  - **The Briar Bar** — famous for the two old men who argue every Thursday.
  - **The Lantern & Lark** — built around a tree growing through the center of the building. Multiple generations have tried to remove it. The tree remains undefeated.
  - **The Sleeping Dragon** — named after a dragon rumored to sleep beneath it. No dragon has ever been found. The owner insists this proves the dragon is still asleep.
  - **Willowmere Tea House** — overlooks the Fairy Meadows. Visitors claim to see unicorns there. The unicorns deny this.

### 6. Royal Post

- **Function:** Mail, letters, parcels, notices, correspondence, eventually bills.
- **Household tie-in:** Check mail daily. Possibly sort bills / pay bills as an administrative sub-branch (TBD).
- **Story role:** Daily light-touch department with strong worldbuilding potential and upcoming-task foreshadowing.
- **Characters:**
  - **Courier Wren** — daily mail courier. Brings letters and kingdom notices. A friendly recurring face.

### 7. Arcane Floorkeepers Guild

- **Function:** Traditional sweeping and floor care. Proud, practical, deeply skeptical of magical cleaning shortcuts.
- **Household tie-in:** Sweep first floor (and other traditional floor work).
- **Story role:** Main flavor comes from its **rivalry with the Witches' Broom Consortium**. They can issue statements, interviews, pamphlets, rebuttals.
- **Characters:**
  - **Guildmaster Thorn** — current head / public voice. Traditionalist. Grumpy about magical shortcuts. Natural rival to the witches.

### 8. Witches' Broom Consortium

- **Function:** Champions magical cleaning advancements, enchanted tools, modern magical convenience.
- **Household tie-in:** Vacuuming / magical floor tools. Bleeds into mirror networks and magical devices.
- **Story role:** Primary rival to the Arcane Floorkeepers Guild. Adds humor and texture to cleaning tasks. Overlaps with the Ministry of Flowing Waters via the Mirror Network.
- **Characters:**
  - **Mistress Thistlewick** — strong recurring witch representative / modernization advocate. Loves the Mirror Network. Associated with the witches' book club. Excellent foil for traditionalists like Thorn and bureaucrats like Brook.

### 9. Ministry of Flowing Waters

- **Function:** All waterworks, reflecting pools, bathhouses, scrying mirrors, fountains, and less glamorous plumbing matters.
- **Household tie-in:** Bathroom cleaning — Clean sink · Clean tub · Clean mirror · Clean toilet.
- **Story role:** One of the richest departments for recurring humor and magical bureaucracy.
- **Sub-locations / task mappings:**
  - **Reflecting Pools** → sink
  - **Royal Bathhouse** → tub
  - **Scrying Mirrors / Mirror Network** → mirror
  - **Deep Pools / Eastern Waterworks** → toilet
  - **Fountain of Whispers** → ongoing lore element (not a scheduled task). Occasionally enters an **Endless Whisper cycle**. Citizens fix it by adjusting the **Silver Listening Chain**.
- **Characters:**
  - **Chief Inspector Minister Brook** — combined Minister + Inspector. Serious, meticulous, overworked. Straight-faced bureaucrat trying to manage magical nonsense.
  - **Apprentice Splash** — junior Ministry employee. Well-meaning, enthusiastic, disaster-adjacent. Always carrying paperwork — always the wrong paperwork. Creates nearly as many problems as they solve, but their heart is too good to replace.
  - **Mistress Thistlewick** — overlaps here via the Mirror Network and book club.
  - **Lord Cush** — minor noble with a beard-mystery problem. Constantly insists the beard trimmings in the Reflecting Pools are not his. Excellent recurring sink-cleaning subplot.
  - **The Apprentice Alchemists** — collective recurring force of chaos. Children using too much soap, bubble potion, bath products. Best left semi-unnamed.

### 10. Knights of Vitality

- **Function:** The kingdom's training order devoted to health, conditioning, strength, and readiness.
- **Household tie-in:** Workouts, exercise days, strength training. Themable by body focus, recruitment, or defense preparation.
- **Rhythm:**
  - **Sunday is the Day of Recovery.** Monday Gazette should mention training resuming after the rest day.
  - Leg day / focused training days are explained through recent skirmishes or identified weaknesses in the ranks.
- **Characters:**
  - The **Master-at-Arms** is the voice of training orders — currently an **unnamed role** (could be named later if a stronger recurring personality is wanted).

### 11. Rangers of the Boundary

- **Function:** Patrols, borders, roaming inspections, outdoor routes.
- **Household tie-in:** Walking / neighborhood walks / weekend patrols.
- **Story role:** Lighter weekly department. Walks can be reframed as patrols, leyline checks, scouting missions, or border reports. Great for weekend cadence. Can tie into Witches' conferences, boundary inspections, or unusual sightings.
- **Characters:** None named yet.

### 12. Royal Gardens Department

- **Function:** Lawns, meadows, flowers, fairy paths, outdoor beauty.
- **Household tie-in:** Mowing the grass · Outdoor upkeep · Flower bed / garden maintenance (later).
- **Rhythm:** Mowing season runs from the week of Easter through the end of October. Needs a long rotation of magical mowing storylines.
- **Story role:** One of the most whimsical departments — fairy / unicorn / flower energy. Meant to feel magical and cute rather than chore-heavy. **Especially tailored to John's daughter** — meant to feel delightful, bright, full of meadow magic.
- **Lore hooks:** Fairy Meadows · unicorn sightings (denied by the unicorns) · whimsical meadow happenings.
- **Characters:** None named yet (a garden steward could be invented later).

### 13. Beastkeeper Association

- **Function:** Grooming and care of royal animals.
- **Household tie-in:** Bathe the dog · Cut dog hair · Other pet care.
- **Story role:** Light, playful animal care. Should feel fun, not like inspection duty. Prompts should feel like: the royal hound must attend a tea party, a princess requires the dog to be presentable, festival appearances, etc.
- **Characters:**
  - **The Royal Hound** — recurring identity (not a person).
  - No named beastkeeper yet.

### 14. Festival Planning / Celebration Committee

- **Function:** Birthday prep, celebrations, cakes, decorations, event planning.
- **Household tie-in:** Birthday prep · Party planning · Advance preparation tasks.
- **Rhythm:** Birthday prep begins about a month in advance.
- **Story role:** Ideal for Gazette foreshadowing — ribbon orders, cake catalogues, invitation notices.
- **Characters:** None yet.

- **The Fountain of Whispers** — Ministry of Flowing Waters feature. Sometimes enters an **Endless Whisper cycle**; fixed by adjusting the **Silver Listening Chain**. Gives cryptic, usually useless phrases (e.g. "The geese know.").
- **Fairy Meadows** — Royal Gardens-adjacent whimsical meadowland. Unicorn-rumor territory.
- **The Briar Bar** — restaurant / tavern with old-man-argument lore.
- **Mirror Network** — magical communication system. Used by witches for book club. Quality suffers when mirrors aren't cleaned.
- **The Battle of Armory** — historic event referenced in tavern lore.

## VI. Story-arc patterns

### Laundry Guild — 5-day arc structure

Each laundry week is **5 days, not 7**. The AI selects an arc from the library below, runs versions 1–5 across the week's five active days, and closes with Mabel's collection bell. The structure stays the same; the reason changes each week.

#### Arc library (seed examples — to be expanded)

**Sheep Shearing Festival Arc**
1. Several guild members are attending the Annual Sheep Shearing Festival to study newly discovered wool varieties.
2. The guild workers have returned from the Sheep Shearing Festival, but Mabel requests quality inspections to ensure the new sorting methods are being followed correctly.
3. Several apprentices have become overly enthusiastic about the new wool classifications learned at the festival and have begun sorting garments into seventeen categories. Mabel requests intervention.
4. A traveling wool merchant has arrived from the northern hills carrying unfamiliar textiles. The guild requires experienced eyes.
5. The festival has concluded, but textile shipments inspired by the event continue arriving throughout the kingdom.

**Great Hiccup Curse Arc**
1. Several Laundry Guild employees have called out sick after contracting the Great Hiccup Curse.
2. The curse has spread to the folding hall. Productivity has suffered.
3. Foreman Phelps attempted to deliver instructions during a hiccup fit. Nobody understood them.
4. The guild reports the curse is subsiding, though several workers continue to hiccup whenever they encounter wool.
5. The Ministry officially declares the outbreak over. The guild is still catching up.

**Porcelain Republic Jury Duty Arc**
1. Several guild employees have been summoned to serve as witnesses in hearings regarding the Great Basin embargo dispute.
2. Additional workers have received jury summons.
3. Testimony has expanded into a second week. Laundry operations continue under reduced staffing.
4. The hearings have concluded. The guild is processing a backlog of garments.
5. The workers return with very strong opinions about basin regulations.

**Missing Sock Investigation Arc** *(6 versions; can run longer)*
1. Routine inventory reveals several missing socks. Head Laundress Mabel opens an investigation.
2. Additional socks disappear. The investigation expands.
3. The guild identifies three primary suspects. No evidence exists against any of them.
4. A reward is offered for information regarding the missing socks.
5. One sock is recovered. Celebrations are brief.
6. Three additional socks vanish. Morale declines.

**Apprentice Faye Certification Arc** *(6 versions)*
1. Faye prepares for her folding certification.
2. Faye has passed the written examination but struggles with fitted sheets.
3. Mabel requests additional mentoring.
4. Faye successfully folds several garments unassisted.
5. Certification day approaches.
6. Faye earns her Folding Certification. Guild celebrations are scheduled.

**Textile Caravan Arc**
1. A merchant caravan arrives carrying a large textile shipment.
2. Additional deliveries arrive unexpectedly.
3. Inventory crews struggle to keep pace.
4. A second caravan arrives. Nobody knows who ordered it.
5. The final shipment arrives. Normal operations resume.

**Royal Festival Preparation Arc**
1. Preparations begin for an upcoming royal celebration. The Laundry Guild expects increased demand.
2. Decorative linens begin arriving.
3. Additional garments require preparation.
4. The celebration approaches.
5. The festival concludes. Recovery operations begin.

**Seasonal Wardrobe Exchange Arc**
1. Citizens begin exchanging seasonal wardrobes. Laundry volume increases dramatically.
2. Storage garments continue arriving.
3. Guild workers report record sorting volumes.
4. The exchange nears completion.
5. Normal garment flow resumes.

**Laundry Guild Training Week Arc**
1. Several senior guild members have been assigned to train new recruits.
2. The recruits show promise.
3. The recruits show confidence.
4. The recruits demonstrate confidence beyond their abilities.
5. Mabel requests additional supervision.

**The Great Linen Census**
1. The Crown has ordered a kingdom-wide linen inventory.
2. Additional records have been requested.
3. The census expands unexpectedly.
4. Several discrepancies have been discovered.
5. The census concludes. Nobody is entirely sure what was learned.

**The Mystery Garment Arc**
1. An unidentified garment arrives at the guild.
2. No citizen claims ownership.
3. The garment becomes a topic of discussion.
4. Several conflicting ownership claims emerge.
5. The mystery remains unresolved.

**Emergency Dye Incident**
1. A shipment of decorative fabrics has leaked dye throughout the sorting hall.
2. Containment efforts continue.
3. The guild requests assistance sorting affected garments.
4. Cleanup operations progress.
5. Normal color levels return.

*(These are seed outlines. The AI beefs them up into full daily Gazette stories at runtime.)*
